Interactive Video: Overview

(Please see the Table of Contents to your left to quickly jump to any Help Topic on this page.)

Interactive Video is a simple and effective way to further 
engage your students in your content and enhance learning. 

Interactive videos are easy to create, edit, and use.

There are more than 15 different kinds of interactions or annotations that can be added.  

  • In-video quiz questions for comprehension checks
  • On-screen links, notes or annotations
  • A built-in end-of-video summary statement (but also a free text tool for those who want students to write their own summary)

This page explains: how to add an interactive video within a lesson and the first steps to begin creating content.

For in-depth information on all 15+ available interactions (what they are, how to use them), head to our "Add Interactions to Interactive Video" Help Page.

1. Add Interactive Video in Lesson

  1. Login to ANVILL Open the ANVILL Lesson in which you want to create a Voiceboard.

  • Click here to learn how to Open an ANVILL Lesson You Created
  • Click here to learn how to Add a New ANVILL Lesson

    2. Once in your lesson page, click on "+ Add Content" at the top of the lesson page. 

    3. Select Interactive Video in the ""Add Content" Menu that opens. An "Interactive Video" will now open on your lesson page for you to create.
  • Note: If you ever create a Voiceboard activity then later want to edit it, just click 
     on the green pencil icon above the video

    4. Continue to Section 2 Below to create the fundamental content for your Interactive Video, then when ready to get started adding interactions, navigate to our Help Page dedicated to Adding Interactions to an Interactive Video.

2. Create Interactive Video Content

To begin:

  1. Open the "Interactive Video Editor." See Section 1 above for instructions.
  2. Give your Video a title. See image above.
  3. Click on the gray box with the plus sign (highlighted in image above) to add your video.
  1. Choose how to add your video:
    • Embed
      a URL link (Vimeo, YouTube, etc)
    • Record 
      video or audio using 
      the internal recorder
    • Upload
      a video or audio file from your computer, phone, or tablet
       
    • Note: ANVILL supports all standard media file options:
      Audio files: m4a, mp3, oga, ogg, wav, wma
      Image files: gif, jpg, jpeg, png, webp
      Video: riles 3gp, avi, m4v, mov, mp4, mpg, mpeg, ogm, ogv, webm

5. It may take your video or audio a moment to upload. Once it has, you will see a black icon appear where the gray box with plus sign was.






 6. After your video has uploaded, add interactions. For help doing so, visit our Help Page dedicated to Adding Interactions to an Interactive Video.

Important note: While most teachers want to use video here, do know that an audio file (a lecture, poem, or newscast off the radio) saved as a movie file can be equally as useful for audio comprehension work. 

3. Create New Interactive Video from Old Interactive Video 

It is possible to create a New Interactive Video from any Existing Interactive Video you have created in an ANVILL lesson. It is a simple and quick "Copy & Paste" process. 

Learn how below:

  1.  Locate the Interactive Video you want to duplicate in your ANVILL Lesson.
  2. Now, open the Interactive Video Editor for that video. If you need to learn how, see Section 1 above.
  3. Once the Editor is open, click on the blue "Copy" button below the title. The content will now automatically copy to your clipboard. 
  4. Click on the green check mark icon above your Interactive Video content to close out of the screen.
  5. Now, click on  "+ Add Content" at the top of your Lesson.
  6. Click on the "Interactive Video" icon in the "Add Content" menu that opens.
  7. The "Interactive Video Editor" screen will now open for your new video. When it does, click on the blue "Paste and Replace" button below the title for your content.
  8. You will be asked for permission to "replace current content with content from your clipboard". Click on "Replace Content" to do so. The Interactive Video you copied earlier, will now paste to this new location.
